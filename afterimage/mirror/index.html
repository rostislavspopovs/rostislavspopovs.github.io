Mirrors (Installation Concept)

Proposal

This is a concept of an installation that I would like to explore. Temporary name “Mirrors”, the audience is invited into a dark room, encouraged to go in by themselves or with their loved one. They see a waving sheet in front of them, on which a glowing pattern is projected. They hear ambient and atmospheric music emanating behind it. As they walk closer, a glowing figure fades in - a mirror image of themselves.

This installation would invite the audience to metaphorically (and physically) face themselves, as if looking through into a mirrored reality. My hope through this work would be to spark an internal and self-reflective dialogue.

Inspiration

There are many such cases in media where the protagonist has to face themselves, admit their mistakes, or learn a sacred truth. I was in particular inspired by a scene from the animated series Legend of Korra, in which Korra (the powerful but deeply hot-headed hero destined to save the world) is placed in a dialogue with herself at her lowest point.

Implementation

  • Input: A depth camera captures and isolates the silhouettes of those in view (this is possible through specialised cameras such as Orbbec; the technology is similar to that of the Xbox Kinect)
  • Through TouchDesigner, the silhouettes are processed, and a background is added. Depth data from the camera can be used to fade the silhouettes in and out depending on each person’s distance from the sheet.
  • The final image is projected onto the sheet.

Additional Considerations

Setting the atmosphere

Although not shown in the above plans, it would be possible to expand the projected pattern effect onto the surrounding walls with the use of additional projectors. These would have to be short-throw projectors so that the person’s shadows are not caught on the walls.

Unequal distances

Should the camera<->sheet and projector<->sheet distances not be equal, it will be possible to adjust the scale and position of the projected image within TouchDesigner to align them 1-1.
