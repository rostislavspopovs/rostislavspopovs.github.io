Experiments in Blender (2026)

No Dogs Allowed In Lake

“NO DOGS ALLOWED IN LAKE” you notice casted on the ground
It sure is effective - no signs of life around
You wonder if that’d be different, would it not be for the
five monoithic steel pillars erected there last summer.

‘No Dogs Allowed In Lake’ was my first experimentation of using multiple media and techniques to create an overarching narrative. The original photo, captured in The Common (Southampton) felt like an interesting base as it captured the signage on the floor while allowing a lot of space for addition.

The statue was 3D scanned with the original installed outside of the Hengistbury Head Visitor Centre in Southbourne. I used the Scaniverse app to create a Gaussian splat representation of the statue by recording it from multiple angles. The original scan can be viewed here (opens in a new tab)

Gaussian splat scanning is unique in that it creates a point cloud consisting of thousands of “splats” meaning that it can not be exported as one mesh. When imported into Blender, it requires a special plugin and shader to assign each point with a colour with shape. As a byproduct of this process, you will commonly find splats floating around the edges of the object and in the air. These are possible to clean up, however I really like the ethereal effect that this gives, as if the model is fading away. For me, it gives the illusion of a phantom, or a crude recollection of a dream. I chose to highlight this effect on the base of the model and to not mention it in the description, allowing the viewer to speculate on its reality.

Tribute to Game Physics

This scene is a tribute to the very iconic yet janky physics that can be found in certain video game engines. In the game Half-Life 2, it is very common for objects to clip into the world, and ss the physics engine tries to push it out, it often over-corrects flinging the object into the air. The spam of sound effects is identical to the one that would accompany it, and it a source of comedy for many players that have experienced this.
